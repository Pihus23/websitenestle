The World's Largest Food and Beverages Company

The History

  • 1860s - Henri Nestlé created one of the first baby formulas. He created a product that was a mixture of cow's milk, flour, and sugar. This was called Farine Lactée. This formula saved many infants lives and prevented them from being malnourished.
  • 1874 -Jules Monnerat bought the Nestlé company. Nestlé then created their own condensed milk to compete with the Anglo-Swiss Condensed Milk Company.
  • 1905 - Nestlé and The Anglo-Swiss Condensed Milk formed a partnership and created factories in the United States, Britain, Spain, and Germany.
  • 1920s - Nestlé started forming powdered beverage products.
  • 1930s - Nescafé was created and became very popular. World War II made it hard for materials to be distributed and so profits decreased.
  • 1947 - Nestlé started partnering with companies that manufactured and produced soups and seasonings.
  • 1960s and 1970s - Nescafé sales went up and they created a new kind of instant coffee.
  • 1980s - Nestlé was thriving and looking to expand the company.
  • 1990s - Trade barriers were taken down and Nestlé was able to trade with parts of countries they couldn't before.
  • Now - Nestlé is still growing and has $81 billion in sales.
